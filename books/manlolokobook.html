...This Isle isn't the stand in for the devil, by the way. Yes, Hell as the depths of the ocean, this guy's name meaning - well, whatever you can glean from the Isle of Deceit... It should be obvious, right? That's not their deal.

They are known as the Manloloko. Residing within the depths of the Marble's oceans, presiding over its life and the aforementioned concept of deceit, he uses the concept in a more positive light - surprises, white lies, hidden generosity and bounty disguised as poison. The benefit side of taking risks, pretty much.

It doesn't preside over the whole of the oceans: she specifically reigns over the Midnight zone and below. He has a love for the deep sea beneath, specifically that of sharks at the moment (previously jellyfish). Another aspect of deceit they embody is a bit of wonder - specifically pointed to unknown monsters in the deep dark, and the myths and tales made from the lack of truth. They still have sadness over the gradual vanishing of awe and appreciation as knowing the unknown causes the effect of disregard e.g. finding the Kraken is a real animal, but handwaving the resulting discovery of the bizarre animals within the depths after being able to record the bottom of the sea.

Its whole thing is being straight up the generic pirate. Even if it doesn't make any sense with the level of depth that she's in, they're highly based on Long John Silver in behavior and tone of voice. Yeah, they'll sound like they're using flattery to the max, and they use it evenly with everyone, but they're 100% being sincere about it even if it sounds like the most face-punch-tempting condescending-sounding 'yeah like you believe what I'm spewing' tone that has ever graced your ears. It then sounds really nice once you understand what's actually going on. Xe legit just cannot help it. It's always gonna sound too good to be true.

Associated with buried treasures, the deep sea ecosystem (obviously), dice and fruit.

They are also based on the Wizard of Oz. Considering their appearance...
