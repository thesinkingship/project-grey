The owner of this pamphlet has no name, currently. They are, also currently, the operator of the ship that houses the shelf that houses the books.
They are extremely indecisive.

The specific concept they associate with is isolation, mostly that of the feeling of exploring empty places, such as those that inspire the feeling of liminality. Because of this, they have little need for such concepts as 'names', and they have difficulty on deciding unless the answer is a no-brainer or with a third option.

They are aware of the irony of being a travelling ship full of passengers. But unlike them and their amiability, they are rather irritable in the presence of others, and can take being truly isolated for months on end.
The other passengers interpret this as denial.

As the Isle of Isolation, they would generally be left alone to their devices, and have a tendency to also get anxious when faced with a situation that relies on communication. Other Isles may aid with such procedures.

Beyond their station, they are generally not observed adventuring through the ship and the world held inside; due to their power being conserved towards piloting the ship, they have little power over changing the landscape to their will.

They are associated with the cards The Fool and The Hermit, alongside fog and low clouds, lanterns, being betwixt and between, crossovers, contrast, coconut shells, gacha capsules and bowls.

Based on Dante from Limbus Company, The Little Prince and Davy Jones from Pirates of the Caribbean. Takes heavy inspiration from the Backrooms, Aperture Science and Myst-like escape room games. They would have taken up other aesthetics, most particularly that of the I-Spy / Mr. Magorium's Wonder Emporium kind, but has supposed that other Isles would make better use of them.

What do they look like, exactly?
Generally it's not really anything of your business.
But it's something of a robot. It is generally understood that they're the embodiment of the ship. Lore states that the Nameless was actually manufactured to be, well, some sorta ship driving robot, which explains the whole lack of identity and not caring if they're alone thing.
Their species? uhhhhh uhhh let's just say they're like. a product of something equivalent to a Magrathean. Said Magrathean-likes went extinct already, this last robot willing to take the planet to its final destination. However, the planet is defective - spatially defective - infinitely bigger on the surface than in containment and from a distance, and so cannot be ejected from the ship at any point, though materials can freely enter, though it's much harder for materials to exit, despite the abundance. It's downright impossible for people to enter and exit, and Isles cannot exit or enter period. Yes, this is an invitation to universe-ending gluttony. Anyways, the Nameless doesn't have much of an interest to do so.

Powers. Powers, powers. Not many, unfortunately. They're an observer.
Their main prowess is just piloting said ship and operating any extensions built - actually turns out they're the whole reason they could modify the size of the ship. It can shrink to about the size of a dandelion seed - yes, the bit on the end of the fluff - up and past the size of the Oort cloud. Time dilation will adjust to an even flow of time calibrated, currently, to Earth humans as a result of the Isle of Novelty showing up. Previously, the ship had been progressing at a sort of accelerated time passage with slowing down in order to dodge obstacles.
The size of the content and therefore passengers inside the ship remain at the same size relative to the ship: should the size be adjusted to the point where the planet shown inside is the same size to the Earth, the people inside remain approximately the same size as the average human, should they decide to present as such.
